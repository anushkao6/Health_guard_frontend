Frequently Asked Questions

FAQs are now part of the Home page. The full list is below for quick reference.

Is HealthGuard AI a doctor?

No. It provides general disease awareness and prevention education. It does not diagnose or prescribe.

Can the chatbot give a diagnosis?

No. The assistant avoids diagnoses and medicine recommendations. Use the information to learn, then consult a clinician for personal evaluation.

How does symptom checking work?

You select common symptoms; the app maps them to broad educational categories and safety tips. Results are not medical advice.

Who can see my chat history?

Only you, when logged in. Messages are stored per user account in the database.

What should I do in an emergency?

Call 112 (India) or your local emergency number. This website is not for emergencies or real-time triage.

What is the report analysis feature?

You can upload a medical report (PDF or DOCX) in the chatbot. The AI will explain findings in plain language — this is educational only and not a substitute for a doctor's interpretation.

Can I delete my account or change my name?

Currently, user accounts can be managed by the system administrator. For profile changes or deletion requests, please contact our support team.

How do I use the location-based health alerts?

Once logged in, the system automatically uses your profile's location or detects it to provide info on Air Quality Index (AQI) and local outbreaks.

Are my reports stored securely?

Yes, reports uploaded for analysis are processed securely and only accessible through your authenticated account. Our system uses industry-standard encryption.

Can I use HealthGuard AI without an account?

Most educational content is available without an account. However, personalized features like location-based alerts and report analysis require a secure login.

Is this service available globally?

While our educational content is general, location-specific features like AQI and specific local health notices are optimized for certain regions as we expand.
